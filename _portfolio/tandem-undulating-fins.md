## Overview

This is my primary M.Eng. research project at USTC, supervised by Assoc. Prof. Fenghua Qin. The goal is to understand how **tandem undulating fins** — inspired by ribbon-fin fish like *Apteronotus albifrons* — generate thrust and how fin–fin hydrodynamic interactions affect overall propulsion efficiency.

## Motivation

Undulating fins offer several advantages over traditional propellers in underwater vehicles:
- High maneuverability at low speeds
- Simultaneous forward/backward thrust without reversing rotation
- Quiet operation suitable for sensitive environments

However, the complex unsteady flow field generated by these fins, especially in multi-fin (tandem) configurations, is not yet well understood.

## Methodology

- **CFD Framework:** OpenLB (Lattice Boltzmann method)
- **Simulation Domain:** 2D and 3D undulating fin models
- **Key Parameters Studied:**
  - Fin spacing (*d/λ*)
  - Phase difference (φ) between leading and trailing fins
  - Strouhal number and reduced frequency
  - Fin geometry (amplitude envelope, wavelength)
- **Validation:** Experimental data from flow visualization and force measurements
