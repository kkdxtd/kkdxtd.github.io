## Overview

This was my undergraduate thesis project at Harbin Engineering University (HEU), supervised by Assoc. Prof. Xuhui Li. The study focuses on the **hydrodynamic loads and green water (wave overtopping) behavior** of a trimaran vessel operating in irregular waves — a critical safety concern for high-speed multi-hull ships.

## Background

Trimarans have become increasingly popular in naval and commercial applications due to their superior resistance and seakeeping characteristics. However, the complex geometry of the cross-deck structure introduces unique challenges regarding:
- Slamming and green water impact loads
- Relative motion of the cross-deck above the waterline
- Structural integrity under extreme wave conditions

## Methodology

- **Numerical Method:** Potential flow theory + CFD (RANS solver)
- **Wave Model:** JONSWAP spectrum for irregular sea states
- **Key Analyses:**
  - Vertical bending moment and shear force along the hull
  - Deck wetness probability under different sea states
  - Comparison of load responses between regular and irregular waves
- **Tools Used:** AQWA (Ansys), custom Python post-processing scripts

## Key Findings

- Green water occurrence increases significantly when the cross-deck clearance falls below a critical threshold relative to significant wave height
- Irregular waves produce higher extreme loads than equivalent regular waves at the same significant wave height
- Identified dominant wave frequency ranges for maximum structural response
